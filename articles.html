Concordia University Nebraska Bulldogs Warm Up For The Season.


Written by Noah Leeper.


The Concordia University Nebraska Bulldogs eSports teams had an eventful week, with strong wins and closes losses it was a competitive week. The Bulldogs showcased their gaming prowess, with Valorant taking center stage.


Strong start for Valorant:


The week started on a high note as the Bulldogs' Valorant team secured a remarkable victory. Facing off against York University, the Team won in 2-0 fashion securing a win first on Haven (13-0) and then on Ascent(13-3). This puts the Bulldogs up 2-0 on the season so far as they look to their week 3 matchup with Kansas Wesleyan Coyotes. The Valorant team led by Captain Tytus Woodburn is heading into week 3 hoping to secure a 3-0 start to their season. Even with starting 2-0 on the season the Bulldogs are going to have a tough season as they participate in the KCAC (Kansas Collegiate Athletic Conference) which has some very tough teams in the future. This seasons wins keep them he current winning streak from last year going as they won their NECC division last year.


Rocket League’s Interesting Week:


The Rocket League team had an interesting week as they participated in both the KCAC and NECC (New England Collegiate Conference) . In the KCAC league, Ottawa university unfortunately had to forfeit their game against the Bulldogs due to scheduling conflicts of their players, as a result this puts The Bulldogs with a 1-1 start to the season following their loss the week before. In the NECC Concordia unfortunately took two losses despite the team's best efforts and Ethan Cronin’s insane double tap goal. However, it isn't all down for the Bulldogs as their sub Stephen Westin played his first competitive game subbing in for Denny Mcatee who sadly was out of town for the weekend. For his first game it was a strong performance he even managed to score a goal during their second game. The Rocket League team finished their Activision games in NECC 2-2. The real action starts in two weeks so the rocket league team has time to rest for that. The Action however never stops in the KCAC as the Rocket League team has a game against Columbia College White who have an extremely strong Rocket League team. The Bulldogs have their work cut out for them this week but with CUNE anything can happen.


Overwatch Misses the Mark:


Unfortunately, the week wasn't without its challenges. The Overwatch team faced an unexpected setback as they missed a crucial deadline, rendering them ineligible to participate in their scheduled match this week. This was a result of a slight misunderstanding with the NECC league however it doesn’t pose as much of a roadblock as this week for overwatch is just Activision games. These games are meant to place the overwatch team into a league that is close to their level so missing this week isn’t a big deal and decisions on league placements will be left up to the Activision games next week.


Expectations for the year:


Concordia University Nebraska's eSports program continues to demonstrate its commitment to excellence in eSports, and their athletes are excited to play this season. The players are hopeful to bring home some trophies for Concordia University this year. The Bulldog’s Valorant team is looking to go far in the KCAC this semester and even further in the NECC next semester. Tune in next week on Concordia Esports Weekly for more CUNE esports news.
